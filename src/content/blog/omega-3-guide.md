Omega-3 products are often sold as if one sentence can capture the whole category. That is rarely a reliable way to read the evidence or the label.

The category blends different fatty acids, different dose ranges, and very different use cases. A reader who sees a sweeping promise should slow down long enough to ask what exact ingredient is being discussed and what outcome is actually being measured.

That habit matters because broad wellness language can make a narrow finding feel universal. Good supplement reporting should shrink claims down to the level where the evidence can actually be inspected.
